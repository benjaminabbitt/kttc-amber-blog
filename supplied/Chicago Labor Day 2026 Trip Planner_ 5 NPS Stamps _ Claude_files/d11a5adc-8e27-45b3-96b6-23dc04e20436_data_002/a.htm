Pullman NHP + Emmett Till & Mamie Till-Mobley NM 2 STAMPS
Pullman National Historical Park
610 E 111th St, Chicago, IL 60628 · (773) 928-7257 · 9AM–5PM daily · Free
The Pullman Administration Clock Tower Building houses both stamps. Exhibits cover labor rights, the Brotherhood of Sleeping Car Porters, immigration, and civil rights. Watch the ranger-narrated film, then walk the Pullman historic neighborhood — the most intact 19th-century planned industrial town in America. The Florence Hotel and Market Hall building are architecturally remarkable.

Emmett Till NM connection: Roberts Temple Church of God in Christ at 4021 S State St (Bronzeville, ~15 min north) is the Chicago site — where the open-casket funeral that changed America was held in 1955. Stamp handled at Pullman VC. Respect the private property of the church.
⚠️Pre-trip: Call (773) 928-7257 to confirm VC is staffed. Past ranger shortages caused unannounced closures.
10:30 AM
Resurrection Catholic Cemetery & Mausoleum

World's Largest Stained Glass Window — 40 feet high, wrapping entirely around the 1969 Resurrection Mausoleum building. Nearly 23,000 sq ft across 2,400 panels depicting the History of Man's Salvation. Walk the exterior first, then enter the building.

Also: home of Resurrection Mary, Chicago's most enduring ghost legend — a young blond woman in white has reportedly hitchhiked on Archer Avenue since the 1930s, vanishing when cars reach the cemetery gate.

Resurrection Catholic Cemetery & Mausoleum
7201 Archer Rd, Justice, IL 60458 · (708) 458-4770
Grounds: 9AM–6PM (Mar–Oct) ✅ · Office: Mon–Fri 8:30AM–4PM, Sat 9AM–1PM

Six Flags Great America — 50th Anniversary Season FULL DAY

1 Great America Pkwy, Gurnee, IL 60031 — Open through Labor Day weekend ✅

2026 marks Six Flags Great America's 50th anniversary — the park opened April 25, 1976. Expect a legacy museum, anniversary brick program, 50th-anniversary merchandise, and special entertainment. This is a genuinely special year to visit.

First-hour priority rides (hit before lines build): Goliath (wooden coaster, world records), Maxx Force (launched, 0-78 mph), Superman: Ultimate Flight, Raging Bull (hyper coaster, 54" req.), American Eagle (classic 1981 racer).

For Honor: Bugs Bunny National Park (dedicated kids' area), Rue Le Dodge (bumper cars), Whirligig, Yankee Clipper (log flume). Goliath requires 48"; Maxx Force and Raging Bull require 54" — verify Honor's height before the trip.

Flash Pass strongly recommended on Labor Day weekend — one of the busiest days of the season. Flash Pass Gold cuts waits to near-zero. Buy at sixflags.com/greatamerica.

Food: Lunch and dinner inside the park. Avoid the main food court. Better bets: churros from street vendors, craft beer garden (adults), chicken tenders near Goliath. Funnel cake is mandatory.

⚠️Pre-trip: Buy tickets and Flash Pass at sixflags.com/greatamerica — 2–4 weeks ahead. Verify Honor's height vs. ride requirements.

Indiana Dunes National Park STAMP
Indiana Dunes NP — Visitor Center
1215 N SR-49, Porter, IN 46304 · (219) 926-2255 · 8AM–6PM (last day of summer hours) ✅
Park activity — Mount Baldy Trail: A 126-foot "living dune" that migrates ~4 feet per year northward, burying trees as it moves. The 0.6-mile trail climbs to the summit with sweeping Lake Michigan views — Chicago skyline faintly visible to the northwest on clear days. The buried "ghost forest" of trees at the base is eerie and beautiful. Free timed-entry pass may be required — check at VC desk for same-day availability.

Alternative if Baldy is full: Cowles Bog Trail (4.7 mi loop) or a quick West Beach walk for a Lake Michigan view. Budget 1–1.5 hrs total.

⚠️ Lunch · Charlie Parker's Diner SPRINGFIELD HORSESHOE
Charlie Parker's Diner — Springfield
700 N St, Springfield, IL 62704 (217) 241-2104 ★ 4.6 Mon–Sun 7AM–2PM ⚠️ ARRIVE BY 1:30
Springfield landmark since 1991 in a WWII Quonset hut. Featured on Food Network Diners, Drive-ins and Dives. 80% of orders are horseshoes.
The Springfield Horseshoe: Two thick slices of Texas Toast, your choice of meat, buried in fries or tater tots, drowned in house-made Welsh rarebit cheese sauce. Request half cheese/half sausage gravy for the classic hybrid. Breakfast Horseshoe (eggs + meat + tater tots + cheese) is the Charlie Parker's signature.
Also order: The 16" pancake (arrives on a pizza-size plate — a genuine spectacle). Pony shoe = half-size version for Honor.
⚠️Closes 2PM sharp. Arrive no later than 1:30 PM and order immediately. Call ahead if running late: (217) 241-2104.

Lincoln Home NHS + Springfield 1908 Race Riot NM 2 STAMPS
Lincoln Home National Historic Site
426 S 7th St, Springfield, IL 62701 · (217) 391-3221 · 9AM–5PM daily ✅ · Free
Both stamps dispensed here — Lincoln Home NHS and Springfield 1908 Race Riot NM. Free timed-entry tickets for the Lincoln Home guided tour at VC desk — take the tour if tickets are available (30–40 min): the only home Lincoln ever owned, furnished as it appeared in 1860.

The surrounding four-block Lincoln Historic Neighborhood is open to walk freely — Dean House, Arnold House, and Corneau House exhibits open 9AM–4:30PM. One of the most intact 19th-century residential urban blocks in America.

Race Riot NM: The monument preserves foundations of five homes destroyed in the 1908 attacks at 9th and 11th / Madison St. The founding of the NAACP grew from national outrage over this event — one year later, in 1909.
